What's OEE?

Overall Equipment Effectiveness. OEE is a measure of how well the assets within a production/manufacturing plant are utilised. It's expressed as a percentage of the plant's full or theoretical maximum capacity and is calculated as follows:

OEE = Availability x Performance x Quality

Where:
Availability = Operating Time / Scheduled Time
Performance = (Parts Produced x Ideal Cycle Time) / Operating Time
Quality = (Units Produced - Defective Units) / Units Produced

OEE is a simple and powerful KPI which can help you to identify the bottlenecks in your production process. Don't upgrade equipment before understanding whether you're addressing the root cause of performance concerns.

The tool below is intended for you if you're new to OEE, and are interested in what your current OEE is likely to be and how if can be improved.


OEE Estimator

Consider your production facility and answer the questions below honestly (none of the data is stored, and you won't be asked for any personal details). Only be answering as honestly as possible will you gain insight into what your OEE is likely to be currently.

Availability

Consider your production process. How many hours a week is it designed to run for? (this is the ideal world figure, so include for scheduled maintenance outages, and rota'd breaks, but not breakdowns or any unplanned events)

Now think about a typical week, how long is production typically stopped during the scheduled running time?


Performance

Maximum throughput under ideal circumstances (never achieved)

This will be compared with the production rate you typically achieve.

Quality

The last input needed, is the number of defects (units which don't meet quality criteria, so need to be scrapped or reworked) experienced at the actual production rate above.
